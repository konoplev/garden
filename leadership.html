Leadership

Nov 2, 2020 #

How to be a leader, build a team, solve conflicts, motivate

Main

A leader is someone who opens new horizons for people.

Cooperation and Intersubjectivity

People cooperate to be more efficient together. To be a company members need to have a common goal and share information efficiently. That’s why transparency, diversity, and inclusiveness are benefitial.

The easier to bring information to the right people, the more efficient the company.

Leadership

It could be split into 3 factors:

  1. People - the ability to find the best team-players, assign roles, delegate, motivate, define culture and principles

  2. Goals - the ability to plan, assign tasks, define processes, monitor progress

  3. Communication - ability to communicate, provide feedback, mentorship, and conflict solving

People

A leader brings a role model. If a leader doesn’t grow, the company doesn’t grow also.

Dictatorship doesn’t work because in such an environment nobody feels safe. People should feel safe to try and fail, otherwise, there will be no motivation to grow.

Truth is more important than revenue. Without truth, there will be no trust. Mistakes shouldn’t be hidden. They should be used to grow. So, feedback is beneficial.

It’s ok to fail sometimes. If you take responsibility (you’re responsible for the result) then it’s ok to fail.

A company should define who we are, what our values are?

The main responsibility of a leader is to provide people with energy. So, a leader should be able to manage energy. For example, a leader should be like a roly-poly toy if any problems are faced (this will be copied by people as well (see role model)).

Hiring

The right "who" will answer to "what" and "how".

Avoid stress during the interview.

Questions (with purpose) for an interview:

Why did you decide to work at X (a company from the CV) - ability to make the right decision What are your achievements (responsibilities) at X - the ability to analyze achievements (understanding responsibilities). How did you deal with the failure? - the ability to take responsibility What would you like to change in yourself - the ability to see weaknesses and improve. And also honesty. What type of people you don’t like? How do you deal with them? - self-control, the ability to be constructive What’s your manager’s/peer/reporter name? If I call him/her how he/she will evaluate you from 0 to 10. What do you need to be 10?

If the answer to 3 is that the problem happened because of someone else this is an indicator that a person can’t take responsibility. The good answer is that I fucked up and I improved something to avoid it in the future.

Hard skills: What’s the most interesting problem you had. How did you solve it? For any answer, ask why did you choose this solution as many times as you need to find which other options were considered and how the decisions were made.

Motivation

Intrinsic motivation should be checked during hiring. It’s not possible to motivate somehow is not motivated at all.

Ways to motivate:

ownership public praise help to grow

1- There is an area of responsibility. A person should understand what is the contribution to the company’s success. Shares(options) is a form of ownership. 2 - We are social animals. It works only when everyone understands what is the achievement. The praise shouldn’t be done often, othervise it will not be valuable. Feedback: if it’s negative the form should be not "You did it wrong", but "I’m upset about results". 3 - it should be clear how to achieve the next level. You can use gamification for that. The next level is more responsibility (see 1), so the higher the level the more possibility to be impactful.

How to kill motivation: Bad environment and unfriendly peers Meaningless tasks

Delegate

Let someone else to perform your task. Otherwise, you don’t let others to grow (and be motivated (no ownership)) and you have less time to grow as well. Set goals not tasks. It’s clear for you != it’s clear for everyone (clarify and motivate (how this goal contribute to company success)) How the result should look like. It should be clear how will we know that the task is achieved. We should have an artifact as a result. When you delegate, you feel that you have less control. But it’s ok, this is how trust is gained. Processes should work without you. Otherwise, a leader is a bottleneck.
